OneArt Photo Studio — Features

🆕 What's New in v7.0

Version 7.0 introduces 35 Photo Styles — a complete library of cinematic, analog, geographic and color-theory presets designed to make AI-generated images look like real photographs. Each style precisely combines film grain, chromatic aberration, color grading, LUTs, and lens character.

🎨 35 Photo Styles — Full Library

🎞 Film Stocks — authentic analog emulations:
• Kodak Portra 400 — warm skin tones, creamy highlights, luminosity grain
• Fuji Pro 400H — pastel cool tones, lifted shadows, dreamy wedding look
• Ilford HP5 B&W — dramatic black & white, deep shadows, visible grain
• Polaroid SX-70 — faded, warm, soft edges, lifted blacks
• 90s Disposable Camera — overexposed flash, lo-fi color shift, hard grain
• Expired Analog Film — color drift, heavy grain, foggy degraded look

☀ Lighting Conditions — real-world light scenarios:
• Golden Hour — warm sunset glow, halation on backlit hair
• Overcast Day — flat diffuse light, muted colors, documentary feel
• Moody Indoor Tungsten — warm orange cast, dark shadows, high ISO noise

🎬 Cinema & Professional — industry camera looks:
• Cinema Log (Flat) — S-Log inspired flat grade, lifted blacks, protected highlights
• Sony Venice Skin — Hollywood cinema skin rendering, subtle halation
• Fashion Editorial — punchy, clean, crisp sharpness, neutral to cool tones

📸 Photography Styles — genre-specific treatments:
• Portrait Studio — clean flash, neutral tones, even exposure, professional
• Street Photography — pushed ISO grain, punchy contrast, Tri-X character
• Travel Documentary — natural saturated colors, clarity boost, honest exposure

🌍 Geographic & Atmosphere — place-specific color:
• Tokyo Neon Night — wet asphalt, cyan/magenta neon, deep shadows
• Havana 1960s — faded tropical warmth, bleached reds, green-cast shadows
• Sahara Desert — scorched ochre, blown highlights, near-monochrome
• Scandinavian Winter — cold blue, flat diffuse, near-white highlights
• Mumbai Monsoon — lush green, humidity haze, warm reflective skin

🕰 Historical Eras — iconic film periods:
• 1970s Kodachrome — punchy saturated reds, road-trip America
• 1990s Point-and-Shoot — oversaturated, harsh flash, purple shadows
• 1940s Noir Newsprint — brutal grain, near-binary contrast, no midtones
• Early Color TV 1960s — bleeding chroma, teal+orange limited palette
• Glasnost USSR 1987 — cold grey-blue, heavy Svema grain, desaturated

🌀 Color Theory — precise colorist techniques:
• Split-Tone Copper & Teal — teal shadows, copper-bronze highlights
• Bleach Bypass — metallic silver, high contrast, desaturated (Saving Private Ryan)
• Orange & Silver — warm skin tones on silver-grey neutrals, luxe editorial
• Cyberpunk Acid — yellow-green + purple, near-black shadows, dystopian
• Muted Earth Tones — sand, clay, olive, slate — only natural pigments

🎞 Director's Eye — iconic filmmaker palettes:
• Wes Anderson Pastel — symmetrical pastel pink/yellow/olive, soft light
• David Fincher Dark — teal shadows, brutal contrast, near-zero light
• Wong Kar-Wai — red+green duality, motion softness, Hong Kong melancholy
• Dune (2021) Sand — near-monochrome warm sand, Villeneuve flat style
• La La Land Golden — pure gold warmth vs cold night blue, vivid contrast

⚡ WebGL-Accelerated Preview

• The entire pipeline is compiled into a GPU fragment shader — 60 FPS lag-free interactive previews across all adjustments.

📀 Tone/RGB Curves

• Cubic spline control over Master RGB, Red, Green, Blue channels. Export to standard 3D LUT (.cube).

🎞 Advanced RAW Develop

• RAW exposure compensation + demosaicing algorithms: AHD, VNG, PPG, Bilinear.

💥 Parallel Batch Processing

• Python multi-processing pool — batch export with real-time progress tracking.

🌡 White Balance

• Manual: Temperature (2000K–10000K) + Tint.
• Auto: Grey World neutralization.
• Smart: Excludes highlights/shadows/saturated pixels, 80% correction to preserve atmosphere.

📷 Style Match

• Pixel Match (CIELAB): Transfer a reference image's color palette at pixel level.
• Slider Match: Auto-analyze reference and tune all sliders to replicate its look.

⌨ Shortcuts

• Ctrl+Z Undo · Ctrl+Y Redo · Space (hold) Compare original
